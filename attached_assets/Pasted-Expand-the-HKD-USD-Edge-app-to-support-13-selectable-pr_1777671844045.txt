PART 2 — SINGLE-MODEL BACKTEST
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Add a new tab called "Backtest." Within it, three sub-tabs:

SUB-TAB A: "Single Model Backtest"

Inputs:
- Date range (default: last 3 years to 30 days ago)
- Forecast horizon to evaluate (1 week / 1 month / 3 months / 6 months — multi-select)
- Selected model (dropdown — all 13 models available)

ENGINE LOOP:
For each historical date D in the range:
  1. Calibrate the selected model using only HKD/USD data through D-1 (no lookahead)
  2. Generate forecast distribution for each horizon (1w, 1m, 3m, 6m forward from D)
  3. Record the forecast distribution
  4. Look up actual HKD/USD rate at D + horizon
  5. Score the forecast (see metrics below)

EVALUATION METRICS for FX forecast accuracy (different from options mispricing — no Sharpe ratio applies because there are no trades, just forecasts):

  - Coverage rate: did the realized rate fall within the model's 70% confidence band 70% of the time? Within 95% band 95% of the time? A well-calibrated model has coverage matching the stated confidence level.
  - Continuous Ranked Probability Score (CRPS): standard measure of probabilistic forecast accuracy. Lower is better.
  - Log score: log of the predicted density at the realized value. Higher is better.
  - Mean Absolute Error of median forecast: how far off was the median prediction?
  - Mean Absolute Error of mean forecast: same for the mean.
  - Calibration plot: predicted probability of being in each band vs. actual frequency of being in that band.

OUTPUT:
- Coverage table: 70% band coverage, 95% band coverage, calibration plot
- CRPS, log score, MAE displayed prominently
- Forecast vs. actual scatter plot (predicted median vs. realized rate)
- Time series chart: actual rate overlaid with rolling forecast distributions over the backtest period
- Verdict panel:
  - "WELL-CALIBRATED — coverage rates match stated confidence levels within 5%"
  - "OVERCONFIDENT — coverage is below stated levels (model thinks it knows more than it does)"
  - "UNDERCONFIDENT — coverage exceeds stated levels (forecasts are too wide to be useful)"
  - "BIASED — forecasts systematically miss in one direction"

SUB-TAB B: "Pairwise Model Comparison"

The user selects EXACTLY TWO models from a dropdown. The backtest runs both models on the same dates, same horizons, same data. Output is a head-to-head comparison view designed for at-a-glance evaluation.

Inputs:
- Date range
- Forecast horizon(s)
- Model A (dropdown)
- Model B (dropdown)

OUTPUT (split view, side-by-side):

Top section — verdict banner:
"WINNER: {Model A or Model B}"
"{Model A} CRPS: {X} | {Model B} CRPS: {Y} | Difference: {X-Y} ({% better})"
"{Model A} Coverage: {X}% | {Model B} Coverage: {Y}% (target: 95%)"
"Statistical significance of difference: p = {p-value} (Diebold-Mariano test)"

Middle section — side-by-side metrics table:
  Metric                | Model A | Model B | Better
  ─────────────────────┼─────────┼─────────┼────────
  CRPS (lower=better)  |   X     |   Y     |  A or B
  Log score            |   X     |   Y     |  A or B
  70% coverage         |   X     |   Y     |  A or B
  95% coverage         |   X     |   Y     |  A or B
  Mean MAE             |   X     |   Y     |  A or B
  Median MAE           |   X     |   Y     |  A or B
  Calibration error    |   X     |   Y     |  A or B
  
  OVERALL WINNER       |         |         |  A or B

Bottom section — visualization:
Two side-by-side charts:
  Left: Model A's actual vs. predicted scatter, with confidence band overlay
  Right: Model B's same chart
Same x and y axis scales for direct visual comparison.

Plus a single combined time-series chart showing actual HKD/USD plus both models' median forecast lines and 70% bands, color-coded by model.

The pairwise comparison must be visually unambiguous — the user should be able to tell within 3 seconds which model performed better on the chosen data.

SUB-TAB C: "All-Model Comparison"

Multi-select checkboxes for any subset of the 13 models (default: all 13). Run all selected models on the same backtest. Output:

- Summary table: one row per model, columns for CRPS, log score, 70% coverage, 95% coverage, MAE, calibration error
- Sorted by CRPS (best at top)
- Statistical significance matrix: Diebold-Mariano test p-values for each pair (triangular matrix)
- Rolling-window CRPS chart: trailing 90-day CRPS per model over backtest period
- "Current Recommendation" panel: best model by recent CRPS, best model by long-term CRPS, most consistent model

PERFORMANCE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

13 models × 3 years × multiple horizons is computationally heavy. Apply:

- Cache HKD/USD price history once, share across all models
- Cache HIBOR / US Treasury yield series once
- Recalibrate complex models (Rough Heston, SVJJ, Double Heston) weekly during backtest
- Recalibrate cheap models (BS variants, Merton) daily
- Parallelize model backtests via multiprocessing
- Use 25,000 Monte Carlo paths for forecast generation during backtest (vs. 50,000 in live mode)
- Progress bar showing per-model status
- First all-model backtest: expect 20–40 minutes; cached re-runs under 1 minute
